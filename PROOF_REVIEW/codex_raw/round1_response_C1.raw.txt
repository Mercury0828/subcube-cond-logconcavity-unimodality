## 2. Frozen substrate — `P*` ("use freely, do not re-derive"; sources in `lit/SCAN_REPORT.md`)
- **P-SR (definition).** `μ` over `{0,1}^n` has generating polynomial `g_μ(z)=Σ_S μ(S)∏_{i∈S}z_i`
  (multiaffine, nonneg coeffs). **`μ` SR ⇔ `g_μ` real stable** (Borcea–Brändén–Liggett, arXiv:0707.2340,
  Def 2.10). Equivalently (Brändén, multiaffine nonneg-coeff criterion) `g_μ` real stable ⇔ the pairwise
  **Rayleigh-difference** `Δ_{ij}(g_μ)(x)=∂_i g·∂_j g − g·∂_i∂_j g ≥ 0` for **all** `x∈ℝ₊^n`, all `i≠j`.
- **P1 (closure).** SR is closed under **subcube conditioning** (fixing coords to 0/1), projection/
  marginalization, and external fields (BBL Prop 2.1 + §4). *(This is exactly what makes the SUBCOND
  oracle natural for SR.)*
- **P2 (negative association).** SR ⇒ CNA+ ⇒ **NA** ⇒ pairwise negative correlation (BBL Thm 4.10).
  🔴 **NA is necessary, NOT sufficient** for SR (no canonical NA-but-not-SR counterexample with a theorem
  number was located in primary text — you may need to construct one; see C0 family F2).
- **P3 (analytic criterion is NOT a local handle).** The Rayleigh-difference criterion holds on the whole
  positive orthant `ℝ₊^n` — a **global analytic** condition, **not** a SUBCOND-observable local statistic.
  🔴 Turning it into a SUBCOND-checkable local quantity (or proving you cannot) is precisely C1's burden.
- **P-Oracle (SUBCOND).** A query fixes a subset `T⊆[n]` to `ρ∈{0,1}^T` and returns a sample from `μ`
  conditioned on `{x:x|_T=ρ}`. `T=∅` = i.i.d. sampling. Cost = number of conditional samples.
- **P-Engine-1 (2502.16355, STOC 2025 — an ENGINE TO ADAPT, NOT a transferring theorem).** Monotonicity
  testing in SUBCOND is `Θ̃(n/ε²)`, tight, via a **real-valued directed Talagrand inequality** (their Thm 3:
  `E_x[(Σ_{i:x_i=−1}((f(x)−f(x^{(i)}))⁺)²)^{1/2}] ≥ Ω(1/√log n)·dist₁(f)`) that converts a **local edge-bias**
  measured on 1-dim subcubes into a global ℓ₁ distance-to-monotone bound. 🔴 **The authors give NO
  extension to SR / log-concavity / negative dependence; the transfer is non-obvious (B3).** Use the
  *bridge architecture* (local statistic → isoperimetric inequality → tester) as inspiration, not the
  inequality itself.
- **P-Engine-2 (2408.02347, APPROX/RANDOM 2024).** SUBCOND subroutines estimating conditional marginals /
  equivalence / product structure in `Õ(n/ε²)` queries — use freely to estimate conditional marginals.
- **P-C0-lean (this project, 2026-06-20 — a *lean*, not a theorem).** On `n∈{3,4}`: the natural pairwise/

**What is proved from P\* facts**

For `mu` with generating polynomial `g`, SR is equivalent to:

\[
\Delta_{ij} g(x)=\partial_i g(x)\partial_j g(x)-g(x)\partial_i\partial_j g(x)\ge 0
\]

for every `i != j` and every `x in R_+^n`.

Equivalently, after applying an external field `lambda`, every pair has nonpositive covariance:

\[
\operatorname{Cov}_{\mu^\lambda}(X_i,X_j)\le 0.
\]

So a mathematically correct global handle is:

\[
V(\mu)=\sup_{i<j,\lambda\in \mathbb R_+^n}
\left(\operatorname{Cov}_{\mu^\lambda}(X_i,X_j)\right)_+.
\]

Then:

\[
V(\mu)=0 \iff \mu \text{ is SR}.
\]

This follows directly from P-SR and the Rayleigh-difference criterion.

**Where the tester argument breaks**

To get Theorem A.2, one would need a quantitative theorem of the form:

\[
d_{\mathrm{TV}}(\mu,\mathrm{SR})\ge \varepsilon
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\exists i,j,\lambda
\text{ such that }
\operatorname{Cov}_{\mu^\lambda}(X_i,X_j)\ge \mathrm{poly}(\varepsilon,1/n),
\]

with `lambda` efficiently searchable or simulable by SUBCOND.

I cannot prove this. It is the missing structural/local theorem.

Even worse, SUBCOND does not directly give samples from `mu^lambda`. Conditioning gives Boolean restrictions, while SR needs all positive external fields. Rejection sampling can simulate bounded-support fields, but in general its acceptance probability can be exponentially small. Thus the analytic SR criterion is global in a way the SUBCOND oracle does not automatically expose.

**CS3: NA-but-not-SR soundness**

A tester based only on pairwise negative correlation, conditional pairwise negative correlation at Boolean subcubes, marginals, or NA-style statistics is not sound.

Reason: P2 gives only

SR \Rightarrow NA,
\]

not the converse. The C0 family F2 already indicates distributions can satisfy pairwise / NA-like visible statistics while failing SR at interior external-field points. Such a tester would accept those distributions and therefore fail CS3.

The only sound route I can state is the external-field Rayleigh tester above: it would reject every non-SR distribution, including NA-but-not-SR ones, because non-SR means some `Delta_ij(g)(lambda)<0`, equivalently some tilted pair has positive covariance. But I do not have a `poly(n,1/eps)` SUBCOND implementation or a robust lower bound on the violation.
