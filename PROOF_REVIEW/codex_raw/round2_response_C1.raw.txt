**P5 Sanity Check**

[Proved.] Let

\[
g(\lambda)=\sum_S \mu(S)\lambda^S.
\]

For positive \(\lambda\),

\[
\mathbb P_{\mu^\lambda}(X_i=1)=\frac{\lambda_i\partial_i g(\lambda)}{g(\lambda)},\qquad
\mathbb P_{\mu^\lambda}(X_i=X_j=1)=\frac{\lambda_i\lambda_j\partial_{ij}g(\lambda)}{g(\lambda)}.
\]

Hence

\[
\operatorname{Cov}_{\mu^\lambda}(X_i,X_j)
=
\frac{\lambda_i\lambda_j}{g^2}
\bigl(g\partial_{ij}g-\partial_i g\,\partial_j g\bigr)
=
-\frac{\lambda_i\lambda_j}{g(\lambda)^2}\Delta_{ij}(g)(\lambda).
\]

So the corrected \(\lambda_i\lambda_j/g^2\) factor is essential quantitatively but irrelevant for sign. Using the frozen BBL/Rayleigh criterion, \(V(\mu)=0\iff \mu\) is SR.

**P6 Sanity Check**

[Proved in principle, not polynomial.] Fix \(i,j\), \(B=[n]\setminus\{i,j\}\). Write

\[
m_t=\mathbb P_\mu(X_B=t),\qquad
q_t(a,b)=\mathbb P_\mu(X_i=a,X_j=b\mid X_B=t).
\]

Then

\[
Z_\lambda=\sum_{t,a,b}\lambda_B^t\lambda_i^a\lambda_j^b\,m_tq_t(a,b),
\]

and the tilted first and second moments are the same weighted sums with factors \(a,b,ab\). Thus \(\operatorname{Cov}_{\mu^\lambda}(X_i,X_j)\) is deterministic post-processing of SUBCOND-estimable conditional tables. No rejection sampling is needed. The obstruction is exactly the exponential sum over \(t\) and possible variance/probability blowup.

**Counterexample To Bounded-Support T**

[Proved.] Let \(\mu_n\) be uniform on the even-parity slice of the cube:

\[
\mu_n(x)=2^{-(n-1)}\mathbf 1\{\sum_i x_i\equiv 0\pmod 2\}.
\]

Its generating polynomial is

\[
g_n(z)=2^{-n}\left(\prod_i(1+z_i)+\prod_i(1-z_i)\right).
\]

Key facts:

[Proved.] Every proper marginal of \(\mu_n\) is uniform product. Any assignment on \(C\subsetneq[n]\) has exactly \(2^{n-|C|-1}\) even-parity extensions.

[Proved.] Therefore if \(\lambda\) has non-unit support \(A=\{k:\lambda_k\neq 1\}\), then for pair \(i,j\), if

\[
A\cup\{i,j\}\neq[n],
\]

the tilted marginal on \(A\cup\{i,j\}\) is still product after reweighting. Hence

\[
\operatorname{Cov}_{\mu_n^\lambda}(X_i,X_j)=0.
\]

In particular, every external field with \(|A|\le n-3\) gives zero covariance for every pair. Thus any \(O(1)\), \(O(\log n)\), or generally \(o(n)\)-support witness family misses \(\mu_n\) completely.

[Proved.] \(\mu_n\) is nevertheless constant-far from SR. Fix pair \(1,2\), condition on \(B=\{3,\dots,n\}\). For every even-parity \(t\), \(\mu_n\) puts mass \(a=2^{-(n-1)}\) on each of \((00,t)\), \((11,t)\), and zero on \((01,t),(10,t)\). For any SR \(\nu\), closure under conditioning and negative correlation imply, on each such face,

\[
\nu(00,t)\nu(11,t)\le \nu(01,t)\nu(10,t).
\]

The \(L_1\) distance from \((a,0,0,a)\) to any nonnegative table satisfying \(uz\le vw\) is at least \(a\). There are \(2^{n-3}\) such even \(t\), so

\[
\|\mu_n-\nu\|_1\ge 2^{n-3}2^{-(n-1)}=\frac14,
\qquad
d_{\mathrm{TV}}(\mu_n,\nu)\ge \frac18.
\]

Thus \(\mu_n\) is \(1/8\)-far from SR.

The full-support witness exists, as P5 requires. If

\[
r_k=\frac{1-\lambda_k}{1+\lambda_k},\qquad R=\prod_k r_k,
\]

then for even parity,

\[
\operatorname{Cov}_{\mu_n^\lambda}(X_i,X_j)
=
\frac{(1-r_i^2)(1-r_j^2)}{4(1+R)^2}
\prod_{k\ne i,j}r_k.
\]

Taking \(\lambda_i=\lambda_j=1\) and \(\lambda_k=\delta=1/n^2\) for \(k\ne i,j\) gives covariance \(\approx 1/4\), but it uses exactly \(n-2\) non-unit coordinates.

**Verdict**

(T) is false under the intended bounded-support/localized interpretation. The explicit family \(\mu_n\) is \(1/8\)-far from SR, yet every field with fewer than \(n-2\) non-unit coordinates has zero tilted pair covariance. So no poly-size \(O(\log n)\)-support localized family can satisfy the claimed robust witness theorem.

This does not refute a different theorem allowing full-support but low-parametric fields; parity is actually caught by a one-parameter full-support tilt. I do not have a proof or refutation of that broader variant here.

No tester/exponent follows from (T), because the stated localization route fails. Confidence: 95% in the derivations and counterexample; 85% that this refutes the intended target as written.
